## Why Reliability Should Be Your Top Priority

When shopping for a used car, reliability isn't just a nice-to-have — it's the single most important factor that determines your total cost of ownership. A reliable used car saves you thousands in unexpected repairs, keeps you safe on the road, and holds its value better when it's time to sell.

In 2026, the used car market offers some incredible options. Many vehicles from 2018–2022 have established solid reliability track records, giving buyers clear data on what to expect. We've analyzed NHTSA complaint data, recall histories, and real-world longevity reports to compile this list.

## The 10 Most Reliable Used Cars in 2026

### 1. Toyota Camry (2018–2023)

The Camry remains the gold standard for midsize sedan reliability. The 2.5L four-cylinder engine is virtually bulletproof, and the eight-speed automatic transmission shifts smoothly for hundreds of thousands of miles. Common issues are minimal — mainly minor infotainment glitches in early 2018 models.

**Expected lifespan:** 250,000–300,000+ miles  
**Average annual repair cost:** $350–$450

### 2. Honda Civic (2019–2023)

Honda redesigned the Civic for 2022, but both generations in this range are excellent. The 2.0L naturally aspirated engine is the reliability pick, though the 1.5T has proven far more dependable than early critics feared. Owners routinely report 200,000+ miles with only basic maintenance.

**Expected lifespan:** 200,000–300,000 miles  
**Average annual repair cost:** $350–$500

### 3. Toyota RAV4 (2019–2023)

The RAV4 dominates the compact SUV segment for good reason. The hybrid version is particularly impressive, combining excellent fuel economy with Toyota's hybrid reliability — a system proven over decades. The 2.5L Dynamic Force engine is one of the most efficient and durable powertrains available.

**Expected lifespan:** 250,000–300,000 miles  
**Average annual repair cost:** $400–$500

### 4. Mazda3 (2019–2023)

Mazda has quietly become one of the most reliable brands on the market. The Mazda3 offers a premium feel at a mainstream price, with Skyactiv engines that are both efficient and long-lasting. The interior quality punches well above its price class.

**Expected lifespan:** 200,000–250,000 miles  
**Average annual repair cost:** $400–$500

### 5. Lexus ES (2019–2023)

Built on the same platform as the Camry but with luxury appointments, the Lexus ES is essentially a more comfortable version of one of the most reliable cars ever made. The ES 300h hybrid is exceptionally efficient and dependable.

**Expected lifespan:** 250,000–300,000+ miles  
**Average annual repair cost:** $450–$600

### 6. Honda CR-V (2018–2023)

The CR-V is one of the best-selling vehicles in America, and its reliability is a major reason why. The 1.5T engine had some early oil dilution concerns in cold climates, but Honda addressed this with software updates. Post-2019 models are especially strong.

**Expected lifespan:** 200,000–300,000 miles  
**Average annual repair cost:** $400–$500

### 7. Toyota Corolla (2020–2023)

The Corolla was redesigned for 2020 with sharper styling and better tech, but Toyota kept what matters most: rock-solid reliability. The Corolla Hybrid is a standout, offering 50+ MPG with virtually no hybrid-specific maintenance concerns.

**Expected lifespan:** 250,000–300,000+ miles  
**Average annual repair cost:** $300–$400

### 8. Subaru Outback (2020–2023)

The Outback offers standard all-wheel drive and rugged capability with solid reliability. The 2.5L boxer engine is the more dependable choice over the turbocharged 2.4T. Ideal for buyers in snowy climates who need capability without sacrificing dependability.

**Expected lifespan:** 200,000–250,000 miles  
**Average annual repair cost:** $450–$550

### 9. Kia Telluride (2020–2023)

Kia's breakout three-row SUV has impressed not just with its value proposition but with genuinely strong reliability. The 3.8L V6 is a proven engine, and early ownership data shows minimal issues. Plus, you may still have warranty coverage remaining.

**Expected lifespan:** 200,000+ miles  
**Average annual repair cost:** $450–$550

### 10. Toyota Tacoma (2018–2023)

If you need a truck, the Tacoma is the reliability king. These trucks are famous for running well past 300,000 miles with basic maintenance. They also hold their resale value better than almost any vehicle on the market.

**Expected lifespan:** 300,000+ miles  
**Average annual repair cost:** $400–$500

## How to Verify Reliability Before You Buy

While brand reputation is a good starting point, every individual vehicle has its own history. A Toyota with deferred maintenance can be less reliable than a well-maintained Chevrolet. Here's how to verify:

1. **Check the NHTSA recall and complaint history** for the specific year, make, and model
2. **Get a pre-purchase inspection** from an independent mechanic ($100–$300)
3. **Run a vehicle history report** to check for accidents, title issues, and service records

## What Makes a Car Reliable?

Several factors contribute to long-term reliability:

- **Engine simplicity:** Naturally aspirated engines with fewer turbo components tend to last longer
- **Proven transmissions:** Traditional automatics and CVTs from Toyota/Honda have excellent track records
- **Build quality:** Tight tolerances and quality materials resist wear
- **Manufacturer track record:** Brands that invest in long-term durability testing produce more reliable vehicles
- **Maintenance accessibility:** Cars with lower maintenance costs get serviced more regularly
